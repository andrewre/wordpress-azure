Coca-Cola

Wimpole Street, London W1

Project Description
The innovative refurbishment of the Edwardian Baroque-style building delivers a highly bespoke flexible workspace that reflects the pop culture heritage of the Coca-Cola company to engage their clients and 300 employees.

Downsizing from their long term Hammersmith base, Coca-Cola’s UK subsidiary had several goals in creating a new headquarters; improving employee engagement, guest experience, brand experience and technology. After stripping the building back to shell and core, a major programme to a unique design was created in only 23 weeks. Rigorous planning and innovative supply chain management were required to support the client sustainability goals to achieve the SKA Gold rating and to deliver the interior designer’s vision. Bespoke major features include a glass and steel feature staircase adjacent to a custom-built double-sided 3-storey wall, that uses lighting and vintage paraphernalia to reflect the brand’s iconic history. An innovative solution was used to create the ceiling design, using felt acoustic rafts made of reclaimed scaffold planks to meet the strict acoustic specifications. A new roof terrace includes a landscaped garden with decking, a meadow and an orchard with apple trees giving Coca-Cola a stunning, unique, productive workplace where teams can come together over a barbeque and share ideas.

Scope and Services
  • High quality Cat B fit out.
  • Refurbishment of 4 floors of bespoke branded open plan offices with hot-desk and breakout areas, 21 flexible meeting rooms, including 9 rooms dedicated to video conferencing, a café and restaurant, new double height reception and atrium, high quality meeting and conference space with presentation cinema and video-conferencing, new staircase and lifts, feature link bridge, picture walls and artwork, café and restaurant area including commercial kitchens, coffee bar and tea points, main mechanical and comms rooms, new showers and changing facility, landscaped roof garden.
Challenges
  • Carefully sequencing the programme to minimise noise and disruption to neighbour.
  • Inventive supply chain management to source over 35 different feature joinery elements including over 120 different finishes, 4 ceiling types, and 12 partition types.
The BW Added Value
  • Delivering SKA Gold using innovative supply chain management Sustainability features include photovoltaic cells on the roof, rainwater harvesting, extensive use of LED lighting and innovative sourcing including timber floors reclaimed from a museum in Scotland.
  • Nominated for the BCO Award for Corporate Workspace (London and South East 2015).
